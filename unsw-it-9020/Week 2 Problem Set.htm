  3. (Mathematical proofs)

    1. Prove that ⌊n2⌋+⌈n2⌉=n⌊n2⌋+⌈n2⌉=n for all integers nn.

      Hint: Give a proof by cases.

    2. Prove that 8∣(n2−1)8∣(n2−1) for every odd number nn (that is, for every n∈ℤn∈Z such that 2∤n2∤n).


  4. (Disjunctive normal form)

    Consider the formulae ϕ1=(r⇒p)ϕ1=(r⇒p) and ϕ2=(p⇒(q∨¬r))ϕ2=(p⇒(q∨¬r)). Transform the formula ¬q⇒(ϕ1∧ϕ2)¬q⇒(ϕ1∧ϕ2) into DNF. Simplify the result as much as possible.


  5. (Karnaugh maps)

    Consider the following canonical DNF of a Boolean function f :

    vwxy′+vwx′y′+vw′xy+v′w′xy′+v′w′x′y+v′wxy′+v′wx′y′+v′wx′yvwxy′+vwx′y′+vw′xy+v′w′xy′+v′w′x′y+v′wxy′+v′wx′y′+v′wx′y

    What is the minimal number of clauses required in any DNF representation of f ? Justify your answer visually by drawing a Karnaugh map.


  6. (Logical reasoning)

    The country of Mew is inhabited by two types of people: liars always lie and "truars" always tell the truth. At a cocktail party the newly appointed Australian ambassador to Mew talked to three inhabitants. Peter remarked that Joan and Shane were liars. Shane denied he was a liar, but Joan said that Shane was indeed a liar. Now the ambassador wondered how many of the three were liars.

    Use propositional logic formulae to help the ambassador.


  7. Challenge Exercise

    Digital circuits are often built only from nand-gates with two inputs. AA nand BB is defined as (A⋅B)′(A⋅B)′, that is, ¬(A∧B)¬(A∧B). Show that nand-gates are sufficient to encode any Boolean function over 2 variables.
